Bowling League
Primary Need: Belonging
Entry Cost

Bowling leagues require a USBC membership ($25/year) plus a weekly lane fee paid to your league. These are fixed costs that don't change week to week.

USBC membership is $25/year (~$2.08/month), already included in the calculation.

Food & Drinks

Most bowling alleys have a bar and grill. Look up your local alley's menu to estimate your typical spend per visit.

Gear & Equipment
Upfront cost shown

You don't need your own equipment to join a league — house balls and rental shoes are available. These costs are for bowlers who want their own gear.

Typical costs: Shoes $150-200 · Entry ball ~$90 · Double roller bag ~$110. Enter $0 if starting with house equipment.

Social Spending

Buying a round for your team is optional but common. Enter $0 if you prefer not to, or estimate your typical spend.

A round of drinks typically runs $20-40 depending on team size.

Timeleft Dinners
Primary Need: Low-Stakes Connection
Membership / Entry Cost

Timeleft requires a subscription to book dinners. Pricing varies by city — US members typically pay between $10.50 and $20 per month depending on the plan length.

Subscription pricing varies by city. Visit timeleft.com for current rates in your area.

Food & Drinks

Your subscription does not include the cost of food or drinks — you pay the restaurant directly. Look up your local Timeleft venue's menu to estimate your typical spend per dinner.
